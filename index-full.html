Introduction

Oil and gas markets at a glance

Canadians depend on pipelines to deliver crude oil, natural gas, natural gas liquids, and refined petroleum products across Canada. These pipelines deliver energy safely, reliably, and efficiently to Canadian end-users, connect North American markets and transport energy to ports for sales overseas.

This report, Canada’s Pipeline Transportation System 2021, uses data from publicly available sources to describe the economic functioning of the major interprovincial and international pipelines regulated by the CER. It also elaborates on the economic regulation of these pipelines, including negotiated settlements and toll proceedings. Finally, it provides an overview of the financial integrity of oil and gas pipeline companies.

For further information, an overview of major CER regulated pipelines can be found in the Pipeline Profiles section of the CER website, and related pipeline throughput and capacity data on the Open Government website. The CER also reports export data for crude oil and petroleum products, natural gas, and crude by rail each month.

Navigating the charts

This report features interactive charts and maps. Here are some tips and an example to get familiar with the interactivity:

  • Downloading the charts: You can download the charts as images in different formats. The data used in the charts can also be downloaded, as CSV or XLS tables. To access these options, click on the three horizontal lines in the upper right corner of the charts.
  • Filtering/selecting Data: Some charts allow you to add and remove data by clicking on the legend item. Some charts also have dropdown filters above the chart.
  • Zooming: Some charts allow you to zoom in on dates. Click on the chart area and drag to the left or right to select a custom date range.
  • Maps: Use your mouse to hover over a map element to view additional info and interactivity.
